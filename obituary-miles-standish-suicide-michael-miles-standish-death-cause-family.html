Miles Standish, Many Career Fields

Michael “Miles” Standish had a distinguished career as an American businessman, author, rare coin expert, sports memorabilia expert, and philanthropist.

His contributions and accomplishments spanned several significant roles within the numismatic and collectibles industry.

In 1984, Standish joined ANACS, America’s Oldest Grading service, as a grader. He later made a significant move to Professional Coin Grading Service (PCGS) in January 1987, becoming their first full-time coin grader.

Standish gained recognition and notoriety for his exceptional skills, including the detection of an impostor coin in 2004.

Throughout his career, he graded an impressive number of coins, estimated to be between 8,000,000 and 10,000,000, which included numerous significant rarities.

Standish’s expertise extended beyond coin grading. He served as the vice chairman and chief numismatist of ANACS, vice president of PCGS, and vice president of Professional Sports Authenticator (PSA).

He also played a pivotal role in the launch of PSA/DNA First autograph authentication. Standish’s contributions to the industry were not limited to his professional achievements.

Miles Standish co-founded Collectors Universe and was also actively involved in philanthropic endeavors.

In addition to his professional accomplishments, Standish authored the book “Morgan Dollar: America’s Love Affair with a Legendary Coin.” It received recognition as a Best Specialized book in the 2015 Annual NLG Writers’ Awards.

Miles Standish’s career was marked by his dedication, expertise, and significant contributions to the numismatic and collectibles industry.

He founded the Standish Foundation For Children with his wife, Andrea. The rare coin expert demonstrated his unwavering dedication to improving the lives of children.

His philanthropic endeavors touched the lives of many and exemplified his generous spirit. Collectors, enthusiasts, and professionals will also remember and celebrate his legacy.

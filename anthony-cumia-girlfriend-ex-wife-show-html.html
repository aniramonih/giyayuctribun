Anthony Cumia Cheated On Ex-Wife; Radio Show Host Bad To Girlfriend Too

American radio personality Anthony Cumia didn't waste a single moment to delve into another relationship after parting ways with his wife, but he couldn't choose a typical affair with his girlfriend, embracing some not-so-nice allegations.

Anthony Cumia is well known for co-hosting the Opie and Anthony radio show alongside Gregg ‘Opie’ Hughes that aired from 1995 to 2014, with Jim Norton joining from 2011.

Anthony Cumia’s Short Bio: 'Twice Fired' Radio Host's Career

Born on 26 April 1961, Anthony Cumia was raised in Elwood, Town of Huntington alongside two siblings, Dawn and Joe.

He started the band, Rotgut with his brother Joe and recorded their first ever song entitled, Gonna Electric Shock OJ, that was a significant hit.

It was in September 1994 when Gregg invited Anthony to the studio to perform the song live and Anthony started to produce comedy bits for Gregg and the station’s morning show soon after.

In March 1995, Anthony and Gregg accepted an offer to host at WAAF in Boston, Massachusetts and launched Opie and Anthony.

The pair got fired in April 1998 for an April Fools Prank that had listeners believe Thomas Menino had died in an accident, transporting a young female Haitian prostitute.

They began at WNEW in June 1998 in New York, becoming a top 10-afternoon drive show in two years.

The show developed when comedian Jim Norton joined as a co-host in 2001 and by 2002 Opie and Anthony syndicated on 17 stations nationwide.

The show got canceled over their ‘Sex for Sam 3’ segment in August 2002 and returned to the air on XM Satellite Radio in October 2004. It was in July 2014 that Anthony got fired by SiriusXM for tweeting about the company as racially-charged and hate-filled.

But Anthony made a comeback to his career as he started his new show, The Artie and Anthony Show in 2017 alongside Artie Lange.

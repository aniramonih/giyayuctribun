Full Overview and Wiki

Early Life and Inspirations

David Strassman, born on September 6, 1957, in Los Angeles County, California, embarked on a journey that would revolutionize the art of ventriloquism. Growing up in a family of four children, Strassman found early inspiration during a family trip to Disneyland, where his fascination with magic and performance sparked.

His father, Harvey D. Strassman, a respected psychiatrist, perhaps unknowingly, laid the psychological groundwork that David would later tap into, bringing depth and nuance to his characters.

Educational Pursuits and the Turning Point

Strassman’s academic path took him to the prestigious American Academy of Dramatic Arts in New York.

However, it was his elective in ventriloquism during junior high school, taught by Chicago children’s TV host Steve Hart, that truly set his course. Despite the allure of acting, ventriloquism captured Strassman’s imagination and ambition, leading him down a path less traveled but filled with potential.

The Early Days: From Busking to Breakthrough

The streets of New York, London’s Leicester Square, and even a brief stint in Paris became Strassman’s initial stages. He honed his craft through busking, connecting with audiences directly and learning the ropes of performance outside the conventional theater spaces.

His appearances in New York comedy clubs and a notable role in Roger Corman’s horror movie Humanoids from the Deep began to establish his presence in the entertainment world.

Technological Innovation and the Birth of a New Ventriloquism

In 1986, Strassman introduced a groundbreaking element to his act – remote-controlled puppets. Drawing from his hobby of flying remote control model airplanes, he equipped his puppet, Chuck Wood, with motors and servos, allowing for a new level of interaction and illusion.

This innovation not only distinguished Strassman’s act but also set new standards in ventriloquism, demonstrating how technology could enhance traditional art forms.

Rising Fame and Critical Acclaim

Strassman’s unique blend of ventriloquism and technology caught the eye of both audiences and critics. His performances on The Late Show with Arsenio Hall and participation in the Edinburgh Fringe Festival, where he received the Critics Award for Comedy, marked him as a leading figure in his field.

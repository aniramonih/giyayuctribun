Wil Wheaton Mental Health Update: Abusive Childhood Trauma, Where Is He Now?

Posted by Patria Henriques on Friday, August 30, 2024

Will Wheaton mental health struggles have hugely affected him for years. Trekkies and other fans are keen to know his latest updates.

The American Actor is best known for playing Wesley Crushe, a “boy genius and Starfleet hopeful,” on the sci-fi series Star Trek: The Next Generation.

His other major roles include Gordie Lachance in Stand by Me, Bennett Hoenicker in Flubber, and Joey Trotta in Toy Soldiers.

Wil regularly appeared as a fictionalized version of himself on the hit sitcom The Big Bang Theory, a show he has thanked for revitalizing his career.

He was also the co-creator and host of the YouTube board game show TableTop and has narrated several audiobooks, including Ready Player One and Ready Player Two.

Wil Wheaton Mental Health Update: Abusive Childhood Trauma

Wil Wheaton lives with mental health issues like chronic depression and generalized anxiety disorder.

Learning from his own experience, he supports mental health nonprofit organizations raising awareness regarding these conditions.

Wil also struggled with alcohol addiction but announced in January 2021 that he had been sober for five years.

As an adult, Wil opened up about his childhood trauma. He described his dad as abusive to him as a kid and his mother as an enabler of the abuse.

The 50-year-old also stated that his parents forced him to become an Actor against his will, which led to multiple mental health issues. He started acting as early as seven years of age. 

His recently released memoir Still Just a Geek reflects his difficult childhood and emotional fallout. He shared that writing the book was often painful and not initially as relieving as he had hoped.

Wil took advantage of the opportunity as a form of therapy. “I have to say it out loud and deal with the reality of it, or I can suffer in silence,” he explained.

To make matters worse, the Star Trek Actor had a health scare when he suffered a horrific “massive, full-body seizure” in August last year. Fortunately, it was not serious, and his health improved with the doctors’ help. 

Where Is Wil Wheaton From Star Trek Now?

As of 2022, Wil Wheaton has been active as an author, a blogger, a narrator, and a radio and podcast host.

He also acts sporadically. His latest appearances include the films S.W.A.T, Star Trek: Picard, the documentary In Search of Tomorrow, and the web show Rival Speak.

Will was also among the participants of the 2022 season of Celebrity Jeopardy! Playing for the National Women’s Law Center, he reached the finals, defeating Joel Kim Booster and John Michael Higgins in the semifinals.

The Stand By Me star has also recently inspired fans through his impressive weight loss. In 2016, he took to his blog to detail his weight-loss journey.

Wil revealed that he had to reset many habits; drink less beer, eat better food, read and write more, and exercise to work on his weight. He also noted that he had taken a year off to spend more time reading and becoming a writer.

Meet Wil Wheaton Family

Wil Wheaton, full name Richard William Wheaton III, was born in Burbank, California, U.S., to his parents Richard William Wheaton Jr. and Debra “Debbie” Nordean (née O’Connor).

His Father is a medical specialist, whereas his mother is an Actress. Will has two younger siblings: 46-year-old brother, Jeremy, and 44-year-old sister, Amy.

Both his siblings appeared uncredited in the “When the Bough Breaks” episode of Star Trek: The Next Generation. Amy also appeared along with Wil in the 1987 film The Curse.

Wil lives in Arcadia, California, with his wife Anne Prince (m. November 7, 1999) and her two sons from a previous relationship. 

Wil has legally adopted both sons: Ryan and Nolan Wheaton.

The Actor is of American nationality, with European descent, including English, Irish, Welsh, German, and French. 

Regarding his religion, he is non-religious and has described himself as an atheist.

Dante Bowe Age, Early Life, Career, Charity Engagements &amp; Net Worth

Posted by Kelle Repass on Friday, August 16, 2024

Dante Bowe, an American Christian worship singer and songwriter, gained recognition as a former member of the Bethel Music collective and Maverick City Music.

He was born on May 10, 1993 (30 years old in 2023) in Rockingham, North Carolina, United States .

His affiliated Record Labels are TRUE Music, Bethel Music.

Notable Instruments are Vocals, Piano.

Dante Bowe Career

Dante Bowe, a GRAMMY Award-winning and multi-platinum-selling artist, has gained recognition for his distinctive raspy vocals and chart-topping hits.

As the CEO of TRUE Music, he has made history with numerous GRAMMY nominations and has taken the music industry by storm.

His soulful mix of gospel and R&B has resonated with audiences worldwide.

Dante’s journey includes chart-topping singles, an independent debut album, and his impact in bridging gaps within the music industry.

In 2017, Bowe marked his musical journey with the release of his debut single, “Potter and Friend,” featuring Jesse Cline.

Soon after, he independently launched his first studio album, “Son of a Father.”

His career took a significant turn when he inked a deal with Bethel Music in June 2019.

The year 2020 witnessed his Bethel Music debut with the single “Champion,” featured on the album “Revival’s in the Air.”

This was followed by three standout singles: “Don’t Talk About It” featuring Jesse Cline,

“Be Alright” featuring Amanda Lindsey Cook, and “Voice of God” featuring Steffany Gretzinger and Chandler Moore.

In 2021, he unveiled his second studio album, “Circles,” which included the chart-topping hit, “Joyful.”

At the 2021 GMA Dove Awards, “Joyful” secured the GMA Dove Award for Contemporary Gospel Recorded Song of the Year.

“Circles” made a grand entrance on the Billboard’s Top Gospel Albums chart in the United States, claiming the No. 7 spot.

Notably, Bowe collaborated on several projects, with one of the most prominent being the song “Wait on You,” alongside Elevation Worship, Maverick City Music, and Chandler Moore.

His remarkable contributions earned him five nominations at the 2022 Grammy Awards;

Culminating in a win for the Grammy Award for Best Contemporary Christian Music Album for his work on “Old Church Basement” (2021) by Elevation Worship and Maverick City Music.

Dante Bowe Charity Engagements

Beyond music, he actively commits to charitable causes, fueled by his passion to create a positive change in the world.

Bowe envisions a future where authenticity in worship unites diverse cultures, generations, and denominations, transcending boundaries.

Dante Bowe Early Life

Dante Bowe was born on May 10, 1993, in Rockingham, North Carolina.

He grew up in a Christian community and his grandparents were Christian ministers.

Bowe’s parents separated when he was eleven years old;

And at twelve, he found out that they were drug dealers in order to sustain their family.

At ten years old, Bowe was molested by a church elder.

Bowe became a converted Christian at sixteen years.

His parents stopped dealing drugs around the same time.

His mother became a converted Christian

At sixteen, Bowe had his spiritual awakening through the music of Kierra Sheard.

He attributed the cultivation of his musical appreciation to his parents.

He went on to develop a love for singing after participating in a seventh-grade talent show.

Bowe dropped out of high school and committed himself to full-time ministry, becoming employed by Eddie James Ministries as a worship leader.

Early Life & Biography

Dana Lynn Eaton was born in Missouri, the United States, on 28 September 1978. She was brought up in a family of Southern Baptists by her mother, Gale. Her paternal grandmother was of Irish ancestry, and she also had a Cherokee ancestry.

For her education, she attended Fox High School in Missouri. After completing her high school studies, she went to St. Louis Community College but later transferred to Webster University, where she majored in journalism. She dropped out of the university at the age of 21 after she got pregnant, and she eventually got married.

Career

The career of Dana started after she left Webster University. She started off by writing a news article for St. Louis Magazine and later started her own website called “Mamalogues.” The website was running weekly by the St. Louis Magazine from 2006 to 2008 as an online column.

In 2008 Dana started hosting her own radio show by the name of The Dana Show: The Conservative Alternative. The show was aired on Radio America, and it became very popular. In 2010 she was hired by Big Journalism as the editor-in-chief. In 2009 Dana co-founded the St. Louis Tea Party together with Bill Hennessy.

She was then hired by CNN in 2011 as a political analyst, and this was in preparation for the 2012 election coverage. Dana has also been a guest host with other national hosts like Glenn Beck and Michael Savage. From January 2014 to November 2017, Dana was hosting a daily television show called Dana on TheBlaze TV.

She was hired as the special assistant to the vice president for the public communication of the National Rifle Association (NRA) in 2017. She became the host of The DL, and this went on until NRA ended the production of the channel in June 2019. As the production ended, she also lost her role as the spokesperson for the NRA.

As an author, Dana wrote her very first book titled Hands Off My Gun: Defeating the Plot to Disarm America. The book was released to the public in 2014. Her second book was released in 2016, and it is titled Flyover Nation: You Can’t Run a Country You’ve Never Been To.

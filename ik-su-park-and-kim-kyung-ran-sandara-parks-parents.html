Ik-Su Park And Kim Kyung-Ran, Sandara Parks Parents

Posted by Tandra Barner on Tuesday, August 27, 2024

The parents of Sandara Park are Ik-Su Park and Kim Kyung-ran. She is from a South Korean family and spent a lot of time there.

Sandara Park is a singer, actress, and TV host who also goes by the stage name Dara.

She gained notoriety in the Philippines as a result of her participation in the Star Circle Quest talent competition in 2004.

Park went on to have a lucrative singing and acting career after the program, and in 2007 he came back to South Korea.

As a member of the K-pop group 2NE1, one of the most successful girl groups of all time until disbanding in 2016, she acquired even more popularity there.

Park was able to concentrate more on her television career during the group’s sabbatical in 2015, appearing as a judge on Pinoy Boyband Superstar and co-hosting Two Yoo Project Sugar Man.

Along with that, Sandara appeared in several movies and web series, including Bcuz of U and Dr. Ian, for which she won several accolades.

Meet the parents and siblings of Sandara Park

Sandara Park was born in Busan, South Korea, to a mother Kim Kyung-ran, and a businessman Father Ik-Su Park.

Her unusual three-syllable given name is based on the “wise and clever” connotation of the 7th-century battle hero Gim Yu-sin’s childhood nickname.

Thunder, 32, and Durami are Sandara’s younger brothers and sisters.

Park Sang-Hyun, aka Thunder, is a model, actor, and singer-songwriter. He is most known for his time spent with the K-pop boy band MBLAQ.

To concentrate on his music studies, he departed the group in December 2014. Later, he joined the music company APOP, owned by Mystic Entertainment, and in December 2016 he released Thunder, his debut extended play (EP).

Thunder has also appeared in several more films, including Woman With A Suitcase, Make a Woman Cry, Welcome to the Show, Padam Padam, and Lovely Voice: The Beginning.

The gifted young performer has also served as a presenter for programs including The Tasty One Shot, The M Wave, Raising Idols, and M! Countdown.

Family Sandara Park lived in the Philippines and South Korea

Sandara Park, a Scorpio by birth, is presently 35 years old and was born on November 12, 1984.

In search of a new beginning and a better life, the Park family first went to Daegu, South Korea, in 1993. However, in 1994, they moved to Alabang, Muntinlupa, Philippines.

Despite being a wealthy business family at first, their father, Ik-Su Park, eventually struggled to make ends meet. He lost a lot of money in his business and filed for bankruptcy.

While attending school there, Sandara met the Filipino actress Pauleen Luna, who convinced her to compete in the singing competition Star Circle Quest 2004.

Despite placing second, she felt discriminated against because of her Korean heritage.

It was her pivotal moment, and she afterward joined Star Magic, an event-planning company that ultimately assisted her in landing several commercials, films, and television shows.

Since she didn’t speak Filipino well at first, Park admitted that she originally felt lonely in the Philippines. Her speech was poorly pronounced as well.

She persisted nevertheless and put a lot of effort into her language with the hopes of achieving fame in the future. Since she first learned about the K-pop boy band Seo Taiji and Boys in 1992, it had been her lifelong desire.

Sandara is now proficient in Tagalog, English, and Chinese in addition to her native Korean.

As of June 2023, the gifted actress and singer is unmarried. According to rumors, Sandara dated rapper G-Dragon, co-actor Kim Soo-Hyun, and Filipino artist Joseph Bitangcol.

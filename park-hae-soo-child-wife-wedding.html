Who Is Squid Game's Park Hae Soo? Net Worth Insights

Park Hae Soo was born on 21 November 1981 in South Korea.

Park Hae Soo started his career in 2007 when he debuted in the musical theatre Mister Lobby. Since then, he has appeared in other musical theatres such as Angel Called Desire, Annapurna, and The Chorus: Oedipus.

Park Hae Soo was part of God Of War, Six Flying Dragons, Legend Of The Blue Sea, and The Liar And His Lover. However, in 2017, Park made a breakthrough as he was cast the lead role in Prison Playbook, winning the Best New Actor award at The Seoul Awards. In Prison Playbook, he played the role of the Basketball player who has anger disorder, and he went to prison for beating his sister's predator to death. 

Park then played a cameo role in drama series such as Memories of the Alhambra and Racket boys. Park also made a significant breakthrough with his latest hit Netflix series Squid Game, where he played an investment banker who is desperate to win the cash. Soon he would appear in the South Korean adaptation of Money Heist, where he plays the role of Berlin.

Besides being cast in drama series, Park Hae Soo also appeared in several movies such as The Pirates, Minority Opinion, Master, Quantum Physics, and Time To Hunt. From the movie Quantum Physics, Park won the Best New Actor at Blue Dragon Film Awards.

It is estimated that Park Hae Soo has a net worth of around $3 million.

Lauren Hashian (born 8 September 1984, Age: 39 years old) is an American singer, songwriter, and producer who is best known as the wife of Hollywood star Dwayne “The Rock” Johnson. Early Life and Family

Lauren was born on September 8, 1984, in Lynnfield, Massachusetts, United States. She is the daughter of Sib Hashian and Suzanne Hashian. Her father was a drummer for the rock band Boston, who replaced the original drummer Jim Masedea in 1975. Her mother was a Dallas Playboy Club Bunny in the 1970s. Lauren has one brother, Adam, and one sister, Aja, who is also a songwriter.

Lauren grew up in a musical household and developed a passion for singing at a young age. She used to play with her sister’s karaoke machine and sing along to songs by SWV and Bobby Brown. She also learned from her father’s dedication and practice as a musician. She attended Lynnfield High School and then graduated from Emerson College in Boston with a bachelor’s degree in public relations and communications.

Career and Achievements

Lauren started her career as a creative synch coordinator at Warner Music Group and later worked for the music department at Paramount Pictures. She also pursued her own musical aspirations and participated in the 2005 reality show R U That Girl, hosted by T-Boz and Chilli of TLC. She made it to the finals but did not win the competition.

She continued to write and produce music with her sister Aja and collaborated with various artists, such as hip-hop artist YaBoi Shad, with whom she co-wrote and sang the song Only You in 2011. She also performed with blues rock band Ernie and the Automatics, where her father was the drummer. She released several songs on SoundCloud, such as Memory, Go Hard, and Samson, featuring Naz Tokio. She also directed the music video for Samson, which was released in 2016.

Hashian has also worked with her husband Dwayne Johnson on some of his musical projects. She co-wrote and sang the song You’re Welcome with him for the Disney animated film Moana in 2016. She also co-wrote and produced the song What a Man Gotta Do with him for the Netflix film Red Notice in 2021.

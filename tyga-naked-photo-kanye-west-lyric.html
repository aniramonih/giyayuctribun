Tyga Shocks Fans With Naked Pose Inspired By Kanye West Lyric

Posted by Patria Henriques on Sunday, August 4, 2024

Tyga has decided to take a Kanye West lyric extremely literally, and the internet is shocked.

The “Rack City” rapper posted to Instagram on Sunday (October 22), captioning a series of photos of himself alone on a beach while quoting Ye’s lyric on “Monster.” “My presence is a present…kiss my ass,” he wrote.

To make his point all the more obvious, the final photo of the four features a bare-bottomed Tyga facing away from the camera.

You can see the shots below.

The bare-assed photo quickly made its way around the internet, and commenters had a field day.

“I think you posted this on the wrong app,” wrote one person, while another joked: “That’s why they call him T-Raw.”

There were also multiple calls for the rapper to delete the photo. “It’s not too late,” one commenter pleaded. Others wondered why he took things this far, with one writing: “I feel like there were better ways to get the message across, maybe?”

Still, more people noticed that Tyga had tattoos in some very private places.

“A grown man with ass tattoos makes me uncomfortable,” someone said.

Some up-on-the-news commenters joked that the photo would work against Tyga in his custody case with Black Chyna. “He gone lose this court case with China for this!” one wrote, while another said: “If I was black china I would use this picture against him in court.”

In between posting racy photos, Tyga is still putting out music. His latest release came at the end of last month, when he and YG dropped Hit Me When U Leave The Klub: The Playlist.

The pair initially announced that the project was on the way at Rolling Loud California in March.

AD LOADING...

“So it’s crazy because last year, or like a year or two ago, we was working on the album, but the shit was going so crazy that we only came up with one song,” Tyga said on stage, with YG in agreement. “But now we about to drop the new album. Coming soon, you feel me?”

The 14-song project features previously-released singles “Platinum,” “PARTy T1M3,” and “West Coast Weekend,” plus collaborations with Lil Wayne, Busta Rhymes and Blxst.

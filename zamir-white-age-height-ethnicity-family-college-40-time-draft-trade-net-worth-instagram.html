The famous Zamir Alexza White, nicknamed Zeus, is an American football running back for the Las Vegas Raiders of the National Football League. He played college football at Georgia and was drafted by the Raiders in the fourth round of the 2022 NFL Draft.

White redshirted his true freshman season after suffering a second torn ACL during summer training camp. White scored the first touchdown of his college career on September 7, 2019, against Murray State in an eight-carry, 72-yard effort in a 63-17 win. He rushed for a season-high 92 yards and one touchdown on 18 carries against Baylor in the 2020 Sugar Bowl. White finished his redshirt freshman season with 408 yards and three touchdowns on 78 carries with two receptions for 20 yards.

In the shortened 2020 season, White was Georgia’s leading rusher with 779 yards and 11 touchdowns on 144 carries. In 2021, White again led all Georgia running backs in a platoon that included James Cook, Kenny McIntosh, and Kendall Milton with 856 yards and 11 touchdowns on 160 carries. On January 14, 2022, White declared for the 2022 NFL Draft.

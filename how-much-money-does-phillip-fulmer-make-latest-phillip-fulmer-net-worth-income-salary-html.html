Phillip Fulmer (born September 1, 1950) is a retired American football player and coach who played in the National Football League. From 1992 until 2008, he was the head coach of the Tennessee Volunteers football team, where he compiled a 152–52 record in that time. He is most remembered for leading the Tennessee Volunteers to a victory against Florida State University in the first-ever BCS National Championship Game in 1998, which was the first of its kind. Fulmer became the Volunteers’ 20th head football coach in their history. Fulmer finished his stint as head coach of Tennessee with the second-highest amount of victories of any head coach in the school’s history, 21 victories behind Robert Neyland.  Fulmer was also the third coach in the history of Tennessee to win a national title that was not contested. His teams from 1997 and 1998 are still the only ones to have won consecutive SEC titles (as of the 2013 season). Despite a decrease in his performance in the latter years of his career, he was widely regarded as an icon of college football, particularly as a symbol of institutional devotion. Fulmer was inducted into the College Football Hall of Fame in 2012, in honour of his achievements while playing for the University of Tennessee. East Tennessee State University’s athletic director has appointed Fulmer to the position of special assistant to the athletic director.

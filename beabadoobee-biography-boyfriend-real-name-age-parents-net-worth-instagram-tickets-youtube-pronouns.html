Biography

Beabadoobee (born Beatrice Kristi Ilejay Laus on June 3, 2000) is a talented Filipino-British singer-songwriter. Her debut single, Coffee, gained significant traction on TikTok and has now exceeded 300 million streams on Spotify.

In 2020, Beabadoobee released her first album, Fake It Flowers, which received critical acclaim and earned a spot on numerous Best Albums of 2020 lists. 

Beabadoobee‘s music blends indie rock with shades of ’90s alternative and grunge, drawing comparisons to artists like Liz Phair and Pavement. Despite her relatively short career, she’s firmly established herself as a rising star in the music industry.

Early Life & Education

Beabadoobee, whose real name is Beatrice Kristi Laus, was born on June 3, 2000, in the picturesque city of Iloilo, Philippines. At the tender age of three, she embarked on a transformative journey when her family relocated to the vibrant neighborhood of Camden in London.

Her familial roots blend Filipino and British heritage, a fusion that would later infuse her music with a rich cultural tapestry. Beabadoobee first embraced the world of melody at 17, when she cradled her first guitar and began crafting her lyrical narratives. Her muses ranged from The Beatles to Elliott Smith and Kimya Dawson. Still, the lo-fi allure of cassette tapes and home recordings truly captivated her artistic spirit.

While she briefly attended Sacred Heart High School, a music college in London, her unwavering passion for music pulled her toward a full-time career in this enchanting realm. Nevertheless, her musical knowledge remains insatiable. Beabadoobee delves into the labyrinth of music theory and production techniques, even venturing into self-production.

Career

2017–2019: Musical Genesis and Recognition

Beabadoobee‘s musical odyssey began with the enchanting Coffee, her debut guitar composition, and a captivating cover of Karen O‘s The Moon Song in September 2017. Coffee garnered over 300,000 YouTube views and caught the attention of Dirty Hit Records, leading to her signing in April 2018. 

This marked the start of her journey, including the release of her debut EP, Lice, in March 2018, followed by Patched Up in December 2018. In January 2019, she earned a spot on NME’s prestigious “NME 100” list alongside stars like Billie Eilish. Beabadoobee‘s artistry flourished with the release of her third EP, Loveworm, and its heartfelt acoustic counterpart, Loveworm (Bedroom Sessions), in July 2019.

2019–2020: Live Performances and Breakthrough

Beabadoobee embarked on her first tour supporting Clairo during the Immunity Tour in September 2019. This journey led to her fourth EP, Space Cadet, and her appearance on the cover of NME in October 2019. 

Her talent was further acknowledged with a nomination for the Rising Star Award at the 2020 Brit Awards. In November 2019, Beabadoobee released Spotify Singles, including a cover of Don’t You Forget About Me and She Plays Bass. The year concluded with her inclusion in the BBC’s Sound of 2020 longlist.

2020–Present: Ascension to Stardom

2020 brought accolades and a chart-topping trajectory. Beabadoobee performed at the 2020 NME Awards and supported labelmates in 1975 on their Music for Cars Tour. Her 2017 single Coffee gained global prominence when sampled in Powfu‘s Death Bed (Coffee for Your Head), a viral sensation on TikTok. This success propelled her into the international charts, and earned numerous certifications.

In July 2020, Beabadoobee unveiled her debut studio album, Fake It Flowers, marked by the enchanting single Care. The album received critical acclaim and secured a place in the UK Albums Chart. Billboard recognized her as the Top New Rock Artist of 2020.

Her journey continued with the single Last Day On Earth in March 2021, leading to her EP Our Extended Play. In March 2022, she announced her second album, Beatopia, released in July 2022.

2023 witnessed Beabadoobee‘s TikTok sensation, Glue Song, and an extensive European tour. She also graced Taylor Swift‘s The Eras Tour as an opening act. Despite canceling her European tour due to illness, Beabadoobee‘s resilience shone through as she announced a new single, “The Way Things Go,” in July 2023.

Personal Life

Beabadoobee was previously in a relationship with Soren Harrison from 2018 to 2021. She is currently dating Jake Erland.

Mason Musso‘s talents extend beyond music, as he has also ventured into solo projects and dabbled in acting, gracing film and television screens. His artistic journey is a captivating blend of melody and performance that continues to evolve with each endeavor.

Early Life

Mason Musso, born on March 17, 1989, in Garland, Texas, grew up in a musical family alongside his brothers, Mitchel Musso and Marc Musso. His father, Samuel Musso, was a jazz musician, and his mother, Katherine Moore, was a singer.

Mason Musso‘s musical journey began early, picking up the guitar at six and composing songs by age nine. In 2006, he and his friend Trace Cyrus formed the band Metro Station, achieving fame with their hit Shake It and releasing multiple albums.

Besides music, Mason Musso dabbled in acting, appearing in TV shows and films. He credits his family for his musical passion and remains grounded despite his success, captivating fans with his catchy tunes and lively performances.

Career

Mason Musso‘s artistic journey is a compelling tale of musical evolution. It all began when fate brought him together with Trace Cyrus, Miley Cyrus‘ older brother, on the set of Hannah Montana. They formed the band Metro Station, sharing their music on Myspace before signing a record deal. Their debut album in 2007 featured the hit single Shake It, which reached the Billboard Hot 100’s top ten and earned them numerous accolades.

Challenges led to a hiatus in 2010, during which Mason Musso pursued solo projects. In 2014, Trace Cyrus returned, and they released their second album, Savior, in 2015. Despite brief hiatuses, the band reunited in 2019, treating fans to the single I Hate Society.

Mason Musso‘s musical prowess extends beyond Metro Station. He collaborated with rapper B.LaY in 2015 and, in 2020, formed Social Order, embracing diverse musical styles. Their debut single, Going Outside Dancing, marked a new creative chapter.

In addition to music, Mason Musso ventured into acting, appearing in TV shows like Hannah Montana and The Suite Life on Deck and the film College Road Trip. His dedication to both music and acting earned him awards and nominations, including a Teen Choice Award and an MTV Video Music Award nomination in 2008.

Early Life & Biography

Hubert Neal McGaughey Jr. was born on 30 July 1958 in Jacksonville, Texas, US. His father was an Irish-American while his mother was a Filipina American. He was inspired by several kinds of music, such as country, rock, disco, and R&B. He used to sing in church choirs before founding an R&B band.

McCoy performed at various clubs and bars in his hometown. While he was in junior college, he used to sell shoes at a shopping mall. In 1981, he won a talent contest and secured a spot as an opening act for Charley Pride.

Personal Life

McCoy married his wife Melinda McGaughey in 1981. They initially met at a shopping mall where he worked in a shoe store. They are the parents of two children, a son named Swayde and a daughter Miki Dougherty.

He is the brother-in-law of country singer Kevin Sharp. Along with his family, McCoy lives happily in Longview, Texas.

Career

Neal McCoy started his musical career in 1988 when he signed with the 16th Avenue Records label. Along with the label, he recorded the singles “That’s How Much I Love You” and “That’s American.”

In 1990, he signed to Atlantic Records when he changed his surname to McCoy. In the same year, he released his debut album “At This Moment.” The lead single of the music album “If I Built You a Fire” reached the Top 20 country hits in Canada. Two years later, McCoy released his second studio album “Where Forever Begins,” which was his first entry on Top Country Albums at No. 58.

In 1994, he released his breakthrough album “No Doubt About It,” which earned a platinum certification from the Recording Industry Association of America (RIAA) and gold certification from the Canadian Recording Industry Association (CRIA). His fourth album, “You Gotta Love That,” also got a platinum certification and had four singles in it. McCoy’s self-titled fifth studio album was certified gold and consisted of singles “Then You Can Tell Me Goodbye,” “Going, Going, Gone,” and “That Woman of Mine.”

In mid-2000, McCoy recorded the music album “24-7-365” along with Giant Records, which had singles “Forever Works for Me,” “Every Man for Himself,” and “Beatin’ It In,” and others. He also signed with SEA Records for a brief period in 2004. The following year, he founded the record label 903 Music with his manager Karen Kane. The first single for the label “Billy’s Got His Beer Goggles On” was released in 2005, which reached the Top 10 on the Hot Country Songs chart. His next release, “The Last of a Dying Breed,” peaked at 36.

In 2008, McCoy with Rhino Records made a compilation album entitled “The Very Best of Neal McCoy,” which had his biggest chart hits. In 2011, he signed with Blaster Music for his twelfth album, XII, which was co-produced by Brent Rowan and Blake Shelton. Later, he recorded the album “Pride: A Tribute to Charley Pride” in collaboration with Darius Rucker, Trace Adkins, and Raul Malo of The Mavericks.

Awards & Achievements

Neal McCoy has won several awards and nominations in his illustrious career. In 1998, he won the “Entertainer of the year award” at the 32nd annual TNN Music City News Country Awards. He has also been awarded The Home Depot Humanitarian Award.

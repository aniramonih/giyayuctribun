Early Life

Cleveland marks itself as the birthplace of the basketball sensation, Charles Oakley. In the early years of his life, he attended a public high school called John Hay High School. He further continued his education at the Virginia Union University.

He played in Division II during his college years and managed to emerge as the lead man in terms of rebounding in his senior year. He performed with an average of 17.3 rebounds per game.

Career

Even though the Cleveland Cavaliers was the first NBA team to pick him in the 1985 draft, Chicago Bulls managed to take away his trade rights. Subsequently, Charles Oakley proved himself as a viable defensive as well as an offensive option on the squad led by Michael Jordan.

From 1985 to 1988, Charles Oakley assisted the team by acting as the ‘cop’. He managed to perform his primary purpose of protecting Jordan against unethical encounters and tackles on the court quite efficiently.

However, in the year 1988, Charles Oakley ended his run with the Chicago Bulls and joined the New York Knicks. That shift in team structures happened when the Bulls traded Oakley for Bill Cartwright.

Later, Oakley established himself as a core member of the Knicks. The 1994 season was the one in which Oakley established a record of 107 starts in a single season.

His role in the Knicks was that of a defensive specialist.

After completing his run with Knicks from 1988 to 1998, Oakley was traded to the Toronto Raptors. The spot he left in the Knicks was filled by Marcus Camby. In the Raptors squad, Oakley acted as an experienced player in a team filled with young rookie blood.

He played for the Raptors from 1998 to 2001. The next step in his career came in the forms of his return to the Chicago Bulls. Out of the 57 games that he played for the Bulls, Oakley started 36 out of them.

After spending a year with the Bulls, Oakley got the chance to play with Michael Jordan yet again when he started playing for the Washington Wizards. That went on for about a year that spanned from 2002 to 2003.

Later, Charles Oakley joined the Houston Rockets in the year 2004. That was the last NBA squad that Oakley was a part of. Rumours surfaced in 2007 about his return, but any follow up did not happen. Oakley himself stated that his return to the NBA wouldn’t come cheap for the teams interested in him.

After ending his career as a professional American basketball player, Oakley signed himself as the assistant coach for the Charlotte Bobcats on December 26, 2010. He resigned that position in the next year owing to health issues.

Awards & Achievements

The year 1986 fetched Oakley the honour of All Rookie-Team. The next significant achievement/award came to Oakley in the year 1994. He was given the All-defensive First team honour for the 1993-94 season.

In the 1997-98 season, Oakley marked the All-defensive second-team honour to his name.

Introducing Tiny Harris

Tameka “Tiny” Harris, born on July 14, 1975, is renowned as an American singer-songwriter from College Park, Georgia. She gained recognition in the 1990s as part of the R&B sensation Xscape, showcasing her distinct vocal talent on hit tracks like “Understanding,” “My Little Secret,” and “Do You Want To.” Her songwriting skills led to a Grammy win for her contribution to TLC’s “No Scrubs.” Beyond music, she made her mark on television through the reality series “Family Hustle.”

Early Life of Tiny Harris

  • Professionally known as Tiny Harris, Tameka Dianne Cottle-Harris was born on July 14, 1975, in College Park, Georgia. Her musical journey began early, driven by a passion for singing nurtured from a young age.
  • At 17, in 1992, Harris ventured into the music industry by joining the R&B group Xscape. Her powerful vocals quickly established her as the lead singer of the group’s hit singles, captivating audiences with her stage presence.
  • Besides her contributions to Xscape, Harris gained acclaim for her songwriting, notably winning a Grammy Award for her work on TLC’s “No Scrubs.” Her family’s ties to the music industry run deep, with her connections to the renowned musical group, the Tams.

Awards and Accolades

Tameka “Tiny” Harris’s musical prowess has earned her several awards, including: 

  • Grammy Award: For writing on TLC’s “No Scrubs” in 2000
  • American Society of Composers, Authors and Publishers Award: In R&B and Pop Music categories for “No Scrubs”
  • ASCAP Rap Award: For her work on the Sporty Thievz track “No Pigeons”
  • ASCAP Rhythm & Soul Music Award: For her contributions to music

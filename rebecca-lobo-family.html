Rebecca Lobo: Family , Net Worth, Husband, &amp; Children

Rebecca Lobo is a renowned name in the world of basketball, having achieved numerous accolades and awards throughout her career. In this article, we will explore her personal life, including her family, husband, children, and net worth, in addition to her remarkable achievements in basketball.

Early Life and College Basketball Career

Born on October 6, 1973, in Hartford, Connecticut, Rebecca Lobo played college basketball at the University of Connecticut from 1991 to 1995. During her time at UConn, Lobo became a standout player on the women’s basketball team, leading the Huskies to an undefeated season and an NCAA championship in 1995.

Professional Basketball Career

After college, Lobo was selected as the number one overall pick in the inaugural Women’s National Basketball Association (WNBA) draft in 1997 by the New York Liberty.

She played for the Liberty from 1997 to 2002, winning the WNBA championship in 1999. Lobo then played for the Houston Comets and Connecticut Sun before retiring in 2003.

Personal Life: Husband and Children

Rebecca Lobo is married to Steve Rushin, a renowned sports journalist and writer. The couple tied the knot in 2003 and has been together ever since. Steve Rushin has written for several publications, including Sports Illustrated and Time magazine.

Together, they have four children: Siobhan Rose Rushin, Thomas, Maeve Elizabeth, and William. Lobo is a devoted mother and frequently shares pictures of her family on social media.

Family Background

Lobo comes from a family of athletes. Her mother, RuthAnn Lobo, played basketball at Southern Connecticut State University, and her father, Dennis Lobo, played basketball at Central Connecticut State University.

Her brother, Jason Lobo, played basketball at the University of New Hampshire, while her sister, Rachel Lobo, played basketball at Salem State University.

Sierra Katow Overview and Wiki

Early Life and Education

Sierra Katow, born in Pasadena, California, to parents Vincent and Corinne Katow, represents a blend of Chinese and Japanese heritage. Her early education at La Cañada Elementary School and La Cañada High School paved the way for her eventual enrollment at Harvard University.

Majoring in Computer Science, Sierra’s academic journey was intertwined with her passion for comedy, evident in her involvement with the Harvard Lampoon and the Harvard College Stand-Up Comic Society.

Early Forays into Comedy

Sierra’s journey into comedy began at a young age. By 16, she was already performing at comedy clubs, showcasing her natural flair for humor.

Her performances were not limited to her hometown; she also graced stages in Boston and Hollywood, especially during her summer breaks from college. This early start laid the foundation for her future success in the industry.

Academic Pursuits and Comedy

While studying at Harvard University, Sierra was actively involved in various comedy groups, including the Harvard College Stand-Up Comic Society.

She also held the position of vice-president of The Harvard Lampoon and contributed as a cartoonist for The Harvard Crimson. Her academic pursuit in Computer Science, coupled with her comedic activities, highlights her multifaceted talents.

Breaking into Television

Sierra’s talent in stand-up comedy led her to television, where she made a significant impact. She was the youngest comedian to feature on Season 9 of Last Comic Standing in 2015, a testament to her exceptional skills.

Additionally, she has performed on Last Call with Carson Daly, Just For Laughs Digital, Laughs on Fox, and Acting Out on MTV, further solidifying her presence in the comedy world.

Venturing into Film and Voice Acting

In 2020, Sierra expanded her repertoire by joining the cast of the Chris Blake quarantine comedy, Distancing Socially. This film, shot during the height of the COVID-19 pandemic, utilized remote technologies and showcased Sierra’s adaptability to different forms of media.

In 2021, she lent her voice to two characters in Disney’s Raya and the Last Dragon, demonstrating her versatility as a voice actor.

Recurring Roles and Writing

Sierra appeared as a recurring character, Evangeline, in Mindy Kaling’s The Sex Lives of College Girls on HBO Max. Her writing skills have also been prominent, contributing to shows like Take My Wife, Drop the Mic, Earth to Ned, and Close Enough on HBO Max, as well as The Wedding Coach on Netflix and Eureka! on Disney Junior.

Cubicall Shark Tank Founder, Net Worth and Investment

Posted by Martina Birk on Tuesday, August 6, 2024

Cubicall, a company known for its innovative modular office design booths, made a splash on Shark Tank, the hit entrepreneurial reality show. In their episode, founders Nick and Anthony Pucci sought a $350,000 investment in exchange for 10% equity in their business.

The Pucci brothers captivated the Sharks with their sleek and customizable booths, which could be tailored to fit various office needs. After a round of intense negotiations, Barbara Corcoran made a compelling offer of $350,000 for 25% equity, and the deal was sealed.

Since their appearance on Shark Tank, Cubicall has faced its fair share of challenges. The company made a strategic move to Nevada, but then the COVID-19 pandemic hit, disrupting businesses worldwide. Despite the obstacles, Cubicall remains resilient and determined to succeed.

Key Takeaways:

  • Cubicall impressed the Sharks on Shark Tank with its modular office design booths.

The Pitch and Negotiation

Nick and Anthony Pucci eagerly presented their innovative modular office design booths during their pitch on Shark Tank. Their ask: $350,000 in exchange for 10% equity in Cubicall.

The sharks were immediately captivated by the product’s potential, recognizing the value it could bring to various workspaces. However, they also had concerns about the company’s financial situation, which led Mark Cuban, Lori Greiner, and Robert Herjavec to drop out of negotiations.

Kevin O’Leary saw an opportunity with Cubicall and made an offer for a royalty deal, hoping to secure a return on his investment. However, Nick and Anthony Pucci believed in the long-term success of their company and were determined to find a partner who shared their vision.

Ultimately, it was Barbara Corcoran who stepped in with an offer that aligned with Cubicall’s needs. She proposed a $350,000 investment in exchange for 25% equity, a deal that the founders eagerly accepted.

The negotiations showcased the sharks’ interest in Cubicall’s innovative product, highlighting its potential and marketability. Barbara Corcoran’s investment not only provided the necessary capital but also brought invaluable expertise and guidance to help Cubicall thrive in the competitive business world.

Post-Shark Tank Update

Since appearing on Shark Tank, Cubicall has experienced its fair share of challenges, particularly in light of the COVID-19 pandemic. Despite these obstacles, the company has persevered and made notable progress. One of the significant changes Cubicall implemented following its appearance on the show was relocating its operations to Nevada. This strategic move aimed to capitalize on the favorable business climate in the state and support future growth.

However, the pandemic presented unexpected hurdles for Cubicall. The global economic downturn and the resulting impact on businesses significantly affected the company’s sales. With a strong belief in its product and the determination to overcome obstacles, Cubicall remains optimistic about future growth and success. The company continues to explore new avenues to boost sales and expand its market reach. By leveraging its expertise in modular office design and adapting to the evolving needs of businesses, Cubicall is well-positioned to drive company growth in the coming years.

In summary, while Cubicall faced significant challenges following its appearance on Shark Tank, the company’s resolve and adaptability demonstrate its commitment to success.

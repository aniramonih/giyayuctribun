Meet Father Duane And Mother Jan

Posted by Billy Koelling on Sunday, August 11, 2024

Alysha Clark attributes all of her success to her parents, Duane and Jan, especially her father.

After the Las Vegas Aces’ WNBA title win, Alysha Clark’s tears held the memory of her late father, Duane, whose spirit fueled her 2023 season. She even dedicates the WNBA title to her late father.

Alysha Clark is a professional basketball player who excels in the WNBA, playing for the Las Vegas Aces and contributing to Elitzur Ramla in Israel.

Her journey to professional basketball stardom began during college, where she achieved an extraordinary feat.

Scoring NCAA charts thrice, she laid the foundation for pro success with exceptional talent and scoring ability.

In 2010, Alysha Clark joined the WNBA, drafted in the second round by the San Antonio Silver Stars.

Her WNBA career took off when she became a key player for the Seattle Storm in 2018.

In that year, the Storm swept the Washington Mystics in the WNBA Finals, earning Clark her first championship with her vital defense and clutch shooting.

Alysha proved herself internationally, earning the 2018 League MVP while with CCC Polkowice in Polish Basket Liga Kobiet Basketball.

In 2019, she clinched another championship, winning the Ligue Féminine de Basketball with Lyon Asvel in France.

Alysha Clark Parents: Meet Father Jan And Mother Duane

Alysha Clark was born on July 7, 1987, in Denver, Colorado, to music enthusiasts parents Jan and Duane Clark, who both loved music.

Her father, Duane Clark, was not only into music but was also a football player. Most importantly, he was a big supporter of Alysha’s sports endeavors.

Unfortunately, Alysha faced a difficult loss when her dad, Duane Clark, passed away in September 2022.

Talking about her other family members, she grew up with an elder sister, Corey Clark, who is popular as an American Idol contestant.

Now, moving towards her origin, Alysha Clark has Jewish roots because her grandparents on her mom’s side could speak Hebrew.

Although she was baptized as a Christian, Alysha’s Jewish heritage became more significant when she played basketball in Israel.

Her family background led her to be recognized as Jewish, and she eventually became an Israeli citizen.

The Clark family moved to Mount Juliet, Tennessee, where Alysha grew up and became skilled in basketball.

Alysha Clark dedicates historic WNBA title to late father

In an exciting Game 4 of the 2023 WNBA Finals, the Las Vegas Aces won their second straight championship by narrowly beating the New York Liberty with a final score of 70-69.

This historic win made the Aces the first team to win two championships in a row since the Los Angeles Sparks did it in 2001-2002.

However, the most touching aspect of this victory extended beyond the realm of sports.

In a heartfelt post-game interview, Alysha Clark expressed how her win was deeply meaningful to her late father, who played a significant role in her life. She dedicated this momentous victory to her dad.

This emotional gesture resonated with WNBA fans and the broader sports community, highlighting the profound impact of personal connections and strong motivations that drive athletes to excel on the grandest stages.

Ultimately, Alysha Clark’s basketball journey is more than just about her skill and global success. It is intricately tied to her family and personal experiences.

Her dedication to the 2023 WNBA title to her late father underscores the profound influence family can have on an athlete’s career and the deep emotions that underlie their achievements.

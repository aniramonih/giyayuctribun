Stuart Yeagley dies at 36 in car accident in Somerset: obituary

Posted by Kelle Repass on Sunday, August 4, 2024

In a heartbreaking turn of events, Stuart Yeagley, 36, lost his life in a fatal car accident on U.S. 40, as reported on Tuesday, December 19, 2023. The news of his untimely demise has left family and friends in deep sorrow, and tributes are pouring in on social media platforms.

The devastating incident came to light through a poignant post, expressing the shock and grief of a family member. The post read, “I’ve been up all night asking why God would take you from this world and your children. There was a car accident on 40 on the scanner yesterday, and never in a million years did I expect I would get a phone call from my cousin telling me it was you and you didn’t survive.”

Survived by a legacy of love and connection, Stuart leaves behind his five children: Katherynn Yeagley of California, Zander Yeagley, Everleigh Vandagriff, Phoenix Vandagriff, and Westin Vandagriff, all residing in Somerset. His memory is cherished by his mother, Janie (Brad) Ervin Smith, and his three sisters: Tiffany James, Kim (Rich) LeFever, and Amber (Thomas) Yeagley. The mothers of his children, Melissa Bennett, Abigail Frick, and Monica Vandagriff, also share in the collective grief.

Stuart’s impact extends to his nieces and nephews: AJ (Brighton) Bryant, Rain (Alex) Wiseman, Ashley James, Noah Miller, and Lucas Wiseman, along with his great-nephew, Arlo Peters. His trusty canine companion, Gonzo, stands as a silent witness to the void left by his departure.

To pay respects and offer condolences, calling hours will be observed at Hoskinson Funeral & Cremation Service, 56 South Main St, Thornville, OH 43076, on Saturday, December 23, 2023, from Noon to 3pm. Green, being Stuart’s favorite color, is encouraged to be worn in his memory.

In lieu of flowers, a trust has been established for the benefit of his children. Donations can be made c/o Hoskinson Funeral & Cremation Services, PO Box 267, Thornville, OH 43076. Additionally, a celebration of Stuart’s life through music will be held at a later date, coordinated with the assistance of Kyle Browning.

This news serves as a somber reminder of the fragility of life, and the community unites in offering support and solace during this challenging time.

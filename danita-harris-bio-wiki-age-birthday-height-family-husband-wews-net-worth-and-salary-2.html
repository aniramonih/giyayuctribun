    Danita Harris WEWS

    Danita is a well-acclaimed journalist with extensive experience in the journalism industry. She is responsible for numerous duties at WEWS. She joined NewsChannel5 as part of the “Morning Exchange,” in 1998 doing live feature reports. Two years later, she moved to WJLA-TV in Washington, D.C where she worked as a weekend anchor also as a weekdays entertainment reporter.

    Nonetheless, there was something extraordinary about Cleveland that she couldn’t resist, perhaps it was the bold she had with the Cleveland community,  she returned back to WEWS in September of 2000 when Danita stepped into the anchor position for “Good Morning Cleveland” and NewsChannel5 at noon. Besides, she is a proud recipient of the 2004 Emmy Award for Best Morning Newscast(Good Morning Cleveland).

    Danita Harris Awards

    Furthermore, Danita also holds two Emmy awards for the best evening newscast and a special on domestic violence in 2017. Moreover, she is also the recipient of the prestigious Chuck Heaton award from The Press Club of Cleveland. Likewise, Danita has been inducted into the Broadcasters Hall of Fame.

    Danita has additionally received three more Emmy Awards for Best Daytime Newscast, Best Evening Newscast, and Breaking News: Caught in the Crossfire – The Shooting Death of 9-year-old Saniyah Nicholson.

    What’s more, Danita reported a 4-part series on the foster care system and won the Journalist of the Year award from the Public Children’s Services Association of Ohio for her work to increase public awareness of child protection.

    For her distinctive work, Danita was recognized by the National Council of Negro Women as one of the Outstanding Women in Ministry in Cuyahoga County. Also, she has had a chance to interview the TV host Oprah Winfrey while working serving as a host on ABC’s “The View”.

    Meanwhile, Danita got promoted to the evening anchor positions she holds today after six years working as a morning show anchor. Besides that, she is active in WEWS’s shadow program that allows high school students to spend the day with broadcast professionals. Danita understands the power of the media and does all she can to wield that power to support young people and push them to success.

    Prior to joining WEWS, she worked at Black Entertainment Television, WJLA-ABC7 in Washington, D.C. During her days in Washington, she used to work as a behind the scenes producer in several areas; news, sports, music, and entertainment

    Danita Harris S.H.I.N.E.

    Aside from finding passion in journalism, Danita is likewise a Rev and an instructor of Effective Communication in Ministry at the Ohio Leadership Academy of the Cleveland Baptist Association. Her latest initiative “It’s Your Time to S.H.I.N.E.” is a women’s group designed to help to hurt women find the light within themselves.

    The group meets monthly with hundreds in attendance. It was birthed out of Danita’s desire to be a figure of hope to those to hopeless, rejected, abused, and hated. Her chief goal is to provide a safe space where women can deal with their issues and get the practical tools they need to overcome personal challenges. In October 2018, she founded the S.H.I.N.E. Girlz Mentoring Program, which is currently in schools in the Cleveland Metropolitan School District.

    During the first year, over 100 4th-8th grade girls successfully completed the S.H.I.N.E. Girlz Mentoring Program. Danita got served in the 8th grade and lives as a woman who continuously seeks the face of God.

3 Suspect Arrested In Rock-Throwing Spree Homicide

Posted by Patria Henriques on Sunday, August 18, 2024

The Alexa Bartell Obituary announced the tragic passing of a bright and caring young woman who lost her life in a senseless act of violence.

The west metro Denver community is mourning the loss of Alexa Bartell, a 20-year-old woman who was tragically killed in a rock-throwing spree that targeted at least seven victims.

Her death has devastated loved ones, and authorities have made three arrests, bringing some measure of justice. But as the investigation continues, questions remain about how such a senseless act could occur.

Alexa Bartell Obituary

Alexa Bartell tragically lost her life on April 19, 2023, when a rock crashed through the window of her vehicle and hit her while she was driving.

Bartell was speaking to a friend on the phone when the incident occurred. The perpetrators of this heinous crime went on a rock-throwing spree that targeted at least seven different victims.

Bartell’s death sent shockwaves through the community and devastated her family, friends, and loved ones. Bartell was described as a bright, caring, and ambitious young woman.

She was known for her love of animals and spent much of her free time volunteering at a local animal shelter. Bartell was also an accomplished artist and loved to paint and draw.

Her family has set up a memorial fund in her honor to support causes that were close to her heart, such as animal welfare and the arts. The senseless act that took Bartell’s life has left many questioning how such a tragedy could occur.

Authorities have made three arrests concerning the incident and are pursuing charges of first-degree murder with extreme indifference. While this brings some closure, it cannot bring Bartell back, and her loss will be felt for years.

Three Suspects Arrested In Rock-Throwing Spree Homicide

Jefferson County sheriff’s investigators arrested three 18-year-old high school friends from Arvada in connection with Bartell’s death. Joseph Koenig, Nicholas Karol-Chik, and Zachary Kwak were taken into custody at their respective homes.

The suspects are believed to have gone on a rock-throwing spree that targeted multiple victims throughout one evening. Bartell was one of at least seven victims targeted in this crime spree.

The suspects allegedly threw large rocks through the windshields of moving vehicles, causing damage and injury. Jefferson County authorities have charged each suspect with first-degree murder with extreme indifference.

Additional charges may be filed pending the results of the investigation. Authorities were able to identify and locate the suspects through mobile device forensics involving cellphone towers.

The investigators received over 300 tips from the public that provided valuable information to aid in the capture of the suspects. The suspects’ families have cooperated with the investigation.

Likewise, their schools have issued statements indicating that they will not return to school by district policy. Mental health support staff with the district is available for students struggling to process the information about the suspects’ arrests.

In addition to the suspects’ arrests, the Father of one of the suspects was also taken into custody for allegedly obstructing deputies. The investigation into this crime is ongoing, and authorities encourage anyone with information to come forward.

In conclusion, the tragic death of Bartell has left a community reeling with grief and shock. Bartell was a promising young woman with her whole life ahead of her, and her death has left her family and friends struggling to come to terms with their loss.

The arrests of the three suspects involved in Bartell’s death bring some measure of justice, but they cannot undo the harm that has been done. It is a reminder that we must all work together to prevent such tragedies from occurring in the future.
